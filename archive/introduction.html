Let's learn how a web page works

What is a web page?

It's just a document with information in it.

Google and Facebook are serving you documents
much like someone emailing you a Word document (just over the web).

Let's see a basic transaction when we visit google.com

First up, does everyone know what a browser is?

Basic web page flow

  1. You to your Browser: "Load google.com"
  2. Your Browser goes off to ask Google for its home page document
  3. Google says "Here's my home page document"
  4. Your Browser downloads it and shows you



Voila, you have now downloaded a web page from google (with perhaps a search query result)



Caveat: there's a lot more going on behind the scenes!

But some of these pages have chat, galleries, animations..?

These are additional components brought in by the document to make it look pretty and add interaction

The main components of a webpage are:

Logo Component Purpose
HTML The content
CSS Makes things pretty
Javascript Makes things interactive

We'll cover what each of those components are when we get into coding later.

Let's adjust the transaction we had before to include loading in additional components into the document

  1. You to your Browser: "Load google.com"
  2. Your Browser goes off to ask Google for its home page document
  3. Google says "Here's my home page document"
  4. Your Browser downloads it and shows you
  5. Your Browser reads through the document and discovers that there's components to load in
  6. Your Browser asks Google for the components
  7. Google says "Sure, here they are"
  8. Browser downloads it and shows you

This transaction can explain why sometimes you get pages that look funny. It could be that you've got the document, but failed to load in some of the other components.
